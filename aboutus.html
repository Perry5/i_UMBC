The idea for this hackathon was the result of an amazing summer that two of the coordinators, Perry Ogwuche and Randi Williams, spent in San Francisco, California as fellows of CODE2040 . They discovered a whole new world of computer science where people sought to solve the problems that revolved around them everyday -- and made successful businesses doing so.

After seeing what computer science could be the two realized that UMBC was a place of untapped potential and decided to host this hackathon to bring hack to UMBC.
Prajit Kumar Das is a Ph.D. student studying Computer Science at UMBC. He is president of UMBC's ACM chapter.
Alexander Morrow is a senior studying Computer Science at UMBC. He is employed with Independent Security Evaluators and president of the UMBC Cryptocurrency Club.
Perry Ogwuche is in his senior year of studying Computer Science at UMBC. He is interested in starting his own company and is already pursuing the opportunity with a project he began over the summer.
Randi Williams is in her sophomore year of study at UMBC. She studies computer engineering and is interested in building products that are not only strong in software but also look and feel cool.
